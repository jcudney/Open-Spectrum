Coming Out: How to tell the people in your life

Coming out can be a stressful and difficult process. Knowing how to approach the conversation, or guide someone you know through the process, can help make the process go far more smoothly.

  • Things to Consider Before Coming Out
  • Tips for Coming Out
  • It is Okay to Stay Private
  • Tips For Supporting Someone Who is Coming Out

Things to Consider Before Coming Out

Before coming out, it’s okay to take time to think through your options and needs. Some helpful questions might include:

  • Is this a safe time and place to share this part of myself?
  • Who do I trust to support me?
  • What do I want to share, and what am I not ready to?
  • How might this change my relationships or environment?

It’s okay to come out in stages, to only tell certain people, or to wait until you feel ready. Safety and mental well-being come first.

Tips for Coming Out

  • Start with Someone Supportive: Telling one trusted person can help you feel less alone and build confidence.
  • Plan Ahead: If you're nervous, write down what you want to say or practice with a friend.
  • User Resources: Coming out letters or similar materials can help you plan out what you'll say or find the words that feel right.
  • Set boundaries: You don’t have to answer every question, and you don’t owe anyone full access to your identity.
  • Prepare for Mixed Reactions: Not everyone will respond perfectly or kindly. That is in no way a reflection of your worth.

It is Okay to Stay Private

Some people choose to never come out publicly, and that’s valid too.

Others choose to stop telling people they're trans once they get to a point where they are considered cis passing. This is referred to by some members of the trans community as being "stealth".

Whether you’re out to everyone, a few people, or just yourself, you are still real, valid, and trans enough.

Tips for Supporting Someone who is Coming Out

If someone comes out to you:

  • Listen with respect and without judgment.
  • Use their chosen name and pronouns.
  • Thank them for trusting you. For many transgender people the process of coming out can be incredibly scary. Taking the steps to come out shows a great deal of trust.
  • Don’t share their identity with others without permission.
